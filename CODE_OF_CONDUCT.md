# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of the **30 Days of Red Team Toolkit** project pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free, educational, and ethical experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community focused on ethical security research and education.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

* **Demonstrating empathy and kindness** toward other people
* **Being respectful** of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* **Giving and gracefully accepting** constructive feedback
* **Accepting responsibility** and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience
* **Focusing on what is best** not just for us as individuals, but for the overall community
* **Using welcoming and inclusive language**
* **Being patient** with new contributors and those learning security concepts
* **Sharing knowledge** and helping others learn
* **Promoting ethical hacking practices** and responsible disclosure
* **Emphasizing authorization and legal compliance** in all discussions

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* **Promoting or discussing illegal activities**, including:
  - Unauthorized access to computer systems
  - Distribution of malware or exploits for malicious purposes
  - Credential theft or sharing stolen data
  - Any form of cybercrime or illegal hacking
  
* **The use of sexualized language or imagery**, and sexual attention or advances of any kind

* **Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments**, and personal or political attacks

* **Public or private harassment** of any kind

* **Publishing others' private information**, such as:
  - Physical or email addresses without explicit permission
  - Credentials, API keys, or authentication tokens
  - Personal details from reconnaissance activities
  - Information obtained during authorized testing without permission to share
  
* **Sharing vulnerabilities or exploits** without following responsible disclosure practices

* **Encouraging or assisting others** in unethical or illegal activities

* **Removing or weakening ethical warnings** and legal disclaimers from code or documentation

* **Using project tools for malicious purposes** or unauthorized testing

* **Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate** in a professional and educational setting

## Ethical Security Research Standards

As a security-focused project, we hold ourselves to additional ethical standards:

### Authorization Requirements

* **Always obtain written authorization** before conducting security testing
* **Never test systems you don't own** without explicit permission
* **Respect scope limitations** defined in testing agreements
* **Stop testing immediately** if you discover you're outside authorized scope

### Responsible Disclosure

* **Follow coordinated disclosure** practices for vulnerabilities
* **Give vendors reasonable time** to patch vulnerabilities before public disclosure
* **Do not weaponize vulnerabilities** or create exploits for malicious use
* **Report vulnerabilities** through proper channels

### Knowledge Sharing

* **Share knowledge for defensive purposes** and education
* **Provide context** about how attacks can be detected and prevented
* **Include blue team perspectives** in discussions
* **Teach responsible practices** to new security professionals

### Legal Compliance

* **Follow all applicable laws** and regulations
* **Understand the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act** (CFAA) and equivalent laws in your jurisdiction
* **Respect intellectual property** and licensing agreements
* **Comply with data protection laws** (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including but not limited to:

* GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, wiki)
* Project Discord server or chat platforms
* Project social media accounts
* Email communications related to the project
* In-person or virtual events representing the project
* Any other forums or platforms where community members represent the project

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include using an official email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, unethical, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at:

**Email:** conduct@30daysofredteam.com  
**Alternative:** security@30daysofredteam.com

**For security vulnerabilities in the project itself**, please use our [Security Policy](SECURITY.md).

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

When reporting, please include:

* Your contact information
* Names (real, usernames, or pseudonyms) of any individuals involved
* Your account of what occurred, including:
  - When and where it happened
  - Whether the incident is ongoing
  - Any relevant screenshots, logs, or links
* Any other information you believe would be useful

All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

### Confidentiality

All reports will be handled with discretion. We will:

* Keep reporter identity confidential unless disclosure is required by law
* Not publicly discuss details without permission
* Only share information with those necessary for investigation
* Protect against retaliation

### Investigation Process

1. **Acknowledgment**: Report receipt acknowledged within 24-48 hours
2. **Initial Review**: Community leaders review the report
3. **Investigation**: Gather additional information if needed
4. **Determination**: Decide if Code of Conduct was violated
5. **Action**: Take appropriate enforcement action
6. **Follow-up**: Check in with reporter about outcome

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, promotion of unethical practices, or sharing malicious content.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals. Also includes promoting illegal hacking activities, sharing tools for malicious purposes, or serious ethical violations.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## Special Enforcement for Security Violations

Given the nature of this project, additional enforcement measures apply for security-related violations:

### Immediate Removal

The following will result in **immediate and permanent ban** without warning:

* **Distributing malware** or malicious code through project channels
* **Sharing credentials or stolen data** obtained illegally
* **Encouraging or assisting illegal hacking** activities
* **Attempting to compromise** project infrastructure or community members
* **Doxxing or sharing personal information** obtained through unauthorized means
* **Using project tools** to conduct unauthorized attacks

### Legal Reporting

In cases involving:

* Illegal activity or cybercrime
* Threats of violence or harm
* Child safety concerns
* Required legal reporting

Community leaders may report incidents to appropriate law enforcement or legal authorities while still protecting reporter privacy where possible.

## Appeals Process

If you believe you have been falsely or unfairly accused of violating this Code of Conduct, you may appeal by:

1. **Sending an appeal** to hello@maxwell-cross.com within 14 days
2. **Including your case** and why you believe the decision was incorrect
3. **Providing any relevant evidence** or context

Appeals will be reviewed by community leaders not involved in the original decision. The appeals process may take up to 30 days.

## Our Commitment to Education

This project exists to **educate** security professionals and enthusiasts. We believe that:

* **Security through education** is more effective than security through obscurity
* **Ethical hackers** play a crucial role in making the internet safer
* **Responsible disclosure** and authorized testing protect everyone
* **Defensive knowledge** comes from understanding offensive techniques
* **The security community** grows stronger through inclusive, ethical collaboration

We welcome all who share these values and commit to learning and practicing security responsibly.
